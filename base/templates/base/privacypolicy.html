Article 11. Cookies and Other Technologies

The Company may use tracking devices such as cookies, IP addresses, or event logs to collect certain information, including the type of browser and operating system, the referrer page, the browsing path through the site, the ISP's domain name, etc., to improve the use and functionality of its Digital Media and to better understand how visitors use the Digital Media, as well as the tools and services offered therein. Tracking technologies allow it to tailor its Digital Media to your personal interests and needs.

Subject to obtaining your consent where required by applicable law, the Digital Media uses cookies and similar technologies ("Cookies"). Cookies are small files installed on your device, which serve various functions, including enabling you to navigate between pages efficiently, remembering your preferences, and generally enhancing your experience. The use of cookies on the Digital Media allows you to navigate more smoothly during your visits and to track your movements on the Digital Media more accurately.

Generally, a cookie contains the name of the website/application from which it originates, the duration for which the cookie will remain on the device, and a value, usually a randomly generated unique number.

An IP address is a number used by computers on the network to identify your computer each time you are connected to the Internet. The Company may store IP addresses (Internet Protocol) in order, in particular, to: (i) solve certain technical problems, (ii) ensure the security of its website, (iii) limit access to its Digital Media to certain users, and (iv) better understand how its Digital Media is used.

The Company (or a third party on its behalf) may collect information in the form of event logs that record the activity of the Digital Media and gather statistics regarding users' browsing habits. These entries are generated anonymously and allow, among other things, to collect (i) the user's operating system and browser type, (ii) information about a user's session (such as the original URL, date and time of consultation of the Digital Media, pages viewed on the Digital Media, and their duration of consultation), and (iii) other similar data regarding the browsing path.

The Company also uses the information contained in event logs for internal marketing and demographic studies, in order to constantly improve and personalize the online services it offers. Event logs are used only internally and are not associated with any particular user. Among the types of cookies that the Company may use are session cookies, persistent cookies, and internal cookies.

  • Persistent cookies are used to save your login data and remember your settings for your next connections to the Digital Media. A persistent cookie is stored as a file on your computer and remains there when you close your web browser. The cookie can be read by the Digital Media that created it when you visit it again.
  • Session cookies are used to enable certain features of the Company's services and Digital Media, to better understand how you interact with the Digital Media, and to track overall usage and Internet traffic routing. Unlike persistent cookies, session cookies are deleted from your computer when you close your browser. They generally store an anonymous session ID on your computer, allowing you to navigate a website/application (for example) without having to log in on each page.
  • Internal cookies are the Company's own cookies, which it uses to enhance your experience. They are associated with an individual user's personal information. No third party can access the information that the Company collects through its own cookies.

Below is a list of the different types of cookies that the Company uses on its Digital Media :

Essential Cookies :
They are absolutely necessary for the proper functioning of the Digital Media and the use of its services and features.
The Company also uses technical cookies that allow the Digital Media to remember your choices (such as your username, language, or country) for customization purposes. These cookies do not collect any information about you that can be used for advertising purposes or to remember the pages you have viewed on the Internet.
These cookies are essential for the proper functioning of the Digital Media. You cannot refuse their use.

Analysis Cookies
The Digital Media may also use analysis cookies (such as Google Analytics). These cookies collect information about how you use and navigate the Digital Media.
For example, these cookies will remember the most visited pages, IP addresses (Internet Protocol), browser type, Internet Service Provider (ISP), referral/exit pages, operating system, timestamp, and browsing path. The Company uses this information to analyze trends, manage the website, track user browsing on the Digital Media, and collect demographic data about the user base as a whole.
You can control cookies through your browser preferences and other tools. To disable these cookies, please refer to our dedicated page on the Digital Media.
